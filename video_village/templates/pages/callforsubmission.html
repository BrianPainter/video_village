CALL FOR SHORT FILM SUBMISSIONS FOR HIGHLAND VIDEO VILLAGE

The City of Spartanburg, SC is one of four cities selected from across the country, to receive up to one million dollars as part of the Bloomberg Philanthropies Public Art Challenge, a new program to support temporary public art projects that celebrate creativity, enhance urban identity, encourage public-private partnerships, and drive economic development. The City of Spartanburg is working with internationally renowned media-based artist Erwin Redl to create nine temporary public art installations across Spartanburg involving the participation of ten neighborhoods and Video Village is one of these installations.

Video Village involves the projection of short films (no longer than 5 minutes in duration) onto the second story windows of vacant housing units in the Highlands Neighborhood in Spartanburg, SC. These short films will be visible during the evening hours and programmed to run in sequence and loops. To learn more about this project click here

Exhibition Dates: October 2, 2016 – April 4, 2017

The Directors of Video Village are seeking short films in the following categories:

  • Abstract Films : often characterized by the absence of linear narrative, the use of various abstracting techniques—out-of-focus, painting or scratching on film, rapid editing. The goal is often to place the viewer in a more active and more thoughtful relationship to the film
  • Animated Films: typically produced by photographing a series of gradually changing drawings, etc. which give the illusion of movement when a series is projected rapidly
  • Documentary Short Films: often examining an event or person based on facts
  • Narrative Short Films: referring to the types of movies that tell a story

Technical requirements

  • Frame Rate: 24 p
  • Aspect Ratio: 4 x 3
  • Total running time not to exceed 5 minutes
  • Audio is not required
  • Codec H.264

Submission Guidelines

  • Completed Application
  • Video Files upload to: videovillage.seeingspartanburg.com

Jury Process

A panel of jurors includes professionals working in the visuals arts and film making industry, members of the Highlands Neighborhood Association and the City of Spartanburg. Selected participants will need to provide further information for this project including a brief biography, photograph of the director, etc. Selected participants will be notified via email or phone. Any questions regarding the submission process can be directed to Elizabeth Goddard, Executive Director of the Spartanburg Art Museum (864) 582-7616 x.236 or egoddard@spartanarts.org
